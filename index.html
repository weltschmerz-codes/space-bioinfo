About This Course

Bioinformatics for Space Biology is a lecture series designed to equip students and early-career researchers with the computational skills needed to analyze real spaceflight omics data. The course bridges biological concepts — from the effects of microgravity on gene expression to protein interaction networks — with practical, hands-on data analysis workflows.

All datasets are real: sourced from experiments conducted aboard the International Space Station, archived in NASA's Open Science Data Repository (OSDR), and freely accessible to anyone in the world.

Who This Is For

  • Students in biology, biochemistry, or life sciences seeking computational skills
  • Researchers new to omics data analysis and bioinformatics pipelines
  • Scientists interested in spaceflight biology and NASA open science data
  • Anyone curious about how real ISS experiment data is analyzed
