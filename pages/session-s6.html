5.1 What Is Differential Gene Expression?

Differential gene expression (DGE) analysis identifies genes whose expression levels differ significantly between two or more biological conditions. In spaceflight research, the typical comparison is: spaceflight samples vs. ground control samples. A gene is called differentially expressed if its expression level is statistically significantly higher or lower in one condition compared to another, after accounting for biological variability.

💡 Key Metrics
  • log2 Fold Change (log2FC): The ratio of expression between conditions on a log2 scale. log2FC = 1 means 2× upregulation; log2FC = -1 means 2× downregulation; log2FC = 2 means 4× upregulation.
  • p-value: Probability of observing the measured difference by chance. Must be corrected for multiple testing.
  • Adjusted p-value (padj): The p-value after Benjamini-Hochberg (BH) correction for false discovery rate (FDR). This is the value to use for filtering significant DEGs.

5.3 Normalization

Raw RNA-seq count data cannot be directly compared between samples because: libraries may differ in total sequencing depth (one sample may have 30M reads, another 50M); longer genes produce more reads simply due to their length; and batch effects may introduce systematic differences. Normalization corrects for these technical factors.

5.4 The Statistical Framework: Negative Binomial Model

RNA-seq counts are not normally distributed — they are discrete, non-negative integers that show overdispersion (the variance is greater than the mean). DESeq2 uses the negative binomial distribution to model count data, which accounts for this overdispersion.

DESeq2's key innovations are: empirical Bayes shrinkage of dispersion estimates (borrows information across genes to improve estimates for genes with few counts) and apeglm/normal/ashr shrinkage of fold change estimates (prevents inflated fold changes for low-count genes).

⚠️ Always Use padj, Not p-value

When testing 20,000 genes, you expect ~1,000 false positives at p < 0.05 by chance alone. The Benjamini-Hochberg adjusted p-value (padj) controls the false discovery rate to 5% across all tests. Always filter DEGs using padj < 0.05, never just p-value. DESeq2 reports NA for padj when a gene has all-zero counts or extreme outliers — these are not significant.

5.6 Standard Visualizations

PCA Plot (Principal Component Analysis): The first check after normalization. Projects samples into a low-dimensional space to reveal overall structure. Biological replicates should cluster together; spaceflight and ground control samples should separate. If one sample clusters with the wrong group, it may be a mislabeled or failed sample.

Volcano Plot: Plots -log10(padj) on the y-axis vs log2FC on the x-axis. Genes in the upper-right quadrant are significantly upregulated; genes in the upper-left quadrant are significantly downregulated. The x-axis threshold is typically |log2FC| > 1; the y-axis threshold is padj < 0.05 (i.e., -log10(0.05) ≈ 1.3).

Heatmap: Shows the expression of selected genes (e.g., top 50 DEGs by padj) across all samples. Reveals whether sample groups cluster by expression pattern, confirms expected direction of changes, and identifies co-regulated gene clusters.

5.7 Case Study: DEG Analysis of Mouse Muscle Spaceflight Data

Using GeneLab dataset GLDS-242 (mice aboard ISS for 35 days, gastrocnemius muscle RNA-seq), a typical DESeq2 analysis would produce approximately: 800–1200 significant DEGs at padj < 0.05 and |log2FC| > 1; enrichment of downregulated genes in myofibril assembly, sarcomere organization, and muscle contraction pathways; enrichment of upregulated genes in protein catabolism, ubiquitin-proteasome pathway, and oxidative stress response. These findings are consistent with the known biology of muscle atrophy in microgravity.

Session Summary

  • DGE analysis identifies genes with statistically significant expression differences between conditions using log2FC and adjusted p-value
  • The standard pipeline: FASTQ → QC → alignment (STAR) → quantification (featureCounts) → normalization → DESeq2 → visualization
  • DESeq2 uses the negative binomial model and empirical Bayes shrinkage to handle small sample sizes
  • Key thresholds: |log2FC| > 1 AND padj < 0.05 define biologically meaningful DEGs
  • Always use padj (not p-value), check PCA first, and filter by baseMean before interpreting results
