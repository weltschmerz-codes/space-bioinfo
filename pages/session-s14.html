📋 About This Worksheet

In this session you will run the full network analysis pipeline from Session 12 on the NASA Twins Study DEG dataset — a different dataset from the Inspiration4 mission used in the lecture, so you can experience the pipeline independently rather than reproducing known results.

You will map DEGs to proteins, construct a PPI network in STRING, analyze it in Cytoscape, identify hub genes using two centrality measures, perform functional enrichment analysis, and write a biological interpretation — exactly the workflow in the PhilSA paper, but applied to your own data and your own findings.

Estimated time: 120 – 150 minutes  ·  You will need: Cytoscape installed, a web browser, and the NASA Twins Study DEG dataset provided by your instructor. Screenshots are embedded directly into this page — click the upload area on each task to add your figure.

How many genes did NOT map? What types of genes are likely excluded and why?
Swiss-Prot only contains entries for protein-coding genes. Non-coding RNAs, pseudogenes, and long non-coding RNAs (lncRNAs) do not produce proteins and therefore have no Swiss-Prot accession — they are automatically excluded from the mapping.

🪐 Part E — Synthesis & Biological Interpretation 0/2

13
Validate
Compare Your Hub Genes to the I4 Mission Results
Cross-study comparison · 15 min

The Inspiration4 mission (3-day civilian flight) identified 8 hub genes: UBA52, FAU, RACK1, RPS2, RPS11, RPS14, RPS15, RPL35 — all ribosomal proteins. Compare your NASA Twins Study hub genes to this list.

13a. Which of your hub genes also appear in the I4 hub gene list? (Overlapping genes)
13b. Which hub genes are unique to your dataset (not in the I4 list)? What might explain why these genes are specific to longer-duration spaceflight?
13c. The I4 mission was 3 days; the NASA Twins Study covers up to 340 days. Does mission duration appear to affect the hub gene landscape based on your comparison? Explain in 2–3 sentences.
Consider: are the overlapping genes (if any) those involved in fundamental cellular stress responses? Are the unique genes in your dataset related to adaptive processes that only emerge over longer time periods — such as immune adaptation, metabolic reprogramming, or cardiovascular changes that take weeks to manifest?
14
Reflect
Write Your Biological Narrative
Scientific writing · 15 min

Write 1–2 paragraphs explaining how the body is affected by spaceflight based on your complete pipeline results. Your answer should reference your specific hub genes, their functional enrichment terms, and what these collectively suggest about the molecular mechanisms of spaceflight adaptation. Connect your findings to what is already known about human spaceflight physiology.

💡 Structure Your Answer
  • Paragraph 1: What hub genes were identified and what functional categories do they belong to? What does this tell us about which biological processes are most disrupted?
  • Paragraph 2: What are the broader health implications? How do your findings relate to known spaceflight effects (e.g. muscle atrophy, immune dysregulation, accelerated aging)? Are your results consistent with the I4 study or do they reveal something additional?
